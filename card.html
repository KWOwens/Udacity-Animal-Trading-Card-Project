Giraffes

Giraffes are the tallest land animals in the world!
Their gestation cycle is about 15 months long. They're born just under 6 feet tall, weighing in at about 143lbs.

  • Scientific Name: Giraffa Camelopardalis
  • Average Size: 14 to 19 Feet Tall
  • Average Lifespan: Up to 26 Years
  • Habitat: Grasslands and Open Woodlands in East Africa

Giraffe's coat colors vary from light tan to practically black. The variations in their color palette are affected by what they eat and where they live. Their unique coat patterns provide them with the ability to camouflage.

Species Controversy: According to the current research of the Giraffe Conservation Foundation (GCF) they strongly conclude that there are four (4) distinct species of Giraffe, with several subspecies. However to the contrary, the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) currently maintains that there is only one Giraffe species, the Giraffa Camelopardalis and nine (9) subspecies.
